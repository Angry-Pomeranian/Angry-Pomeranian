### About Me

*An Information Security Officer at an ISO 27001 certified Australian MSP.*

*My path here was a proper career pivot -> bartending to cybersecurity, which sounds hectic but honestly both jobs are just figuring out what's actually going on and keeping things from going sideways.*

*Fell into this industry during lockdown 6 during COVID and just kept going deeper. Somewhere along the way compliance went from something I just put up with to something I'm actually pretty stoked about, which I reckon is not the most common trajectory.*

*My work is mostly in detection engineering, identity architecture, cloud security, and AI governance; but GRC overall is a space I'm very comfortable in. Been drifting into general DevSecOps and DevOps territory lately, especially in regards to automation (Event-Driven Ansible (EDA) and pre-EDA). My security portfolio is where a lot of my docs and work lives, so go have a dig around.*

*Outside of work my hobbies are reading, writing, and video games. I've also really started to deep dive into a few open source projects that I plan on trying out this year.*

*Specifically a few big items on the roadmap:*

- *I'm building a custom portable cyberdeck powered by a Raspberry Pi 5 with an on-device AI accelerator, designed for wireless security research and penetration testing. It combines a purpose-built ESP32 co-processor for RF attacks across WiFi, Bluetooth, Sub-GHz, and infrared with a full Kali Linux toolset and a local LLM that can assist with reconnaissance and attack chain planning in the field.*

- *Creating my own cellphone! I had come across this project: [A Modified zFlip 5](https://www.pcbway.com/project/shareproject/A_Modified_zFlip_5_969c2c62.html), which if you haven't seen the video I highly recommend checking out. Got inspired to attempt making my own smartphone. I plan for this to be after I make the cyberdeck, but am still looking for inspiration on some cool features to try and include. If you have any recommendations feel free to shoot me a message!*
